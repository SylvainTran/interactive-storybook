Dyerden

The rain hath a cold promise made to wash away my fear.

In my childhood it swore thus, and in my adulthood it remaineth like a dog.

Yet instead of my redemption obtained, my irk grows by the year.

So my body continues to scorch and to relieve and to feel as sog.

How often hath the rain been slippery and a liar most jesterly?


A heart of dust

Whence shall I find true love and destiny?

Should I labor for no merit and only calamity?

Hath my blacksmith forged a blade unsheathed and made to rust?

If no rivers be strong enough for this heart of dust,

and until my soul rejoices of having a respite of honey and dew found,

my spirit shall not rest.


Attachments


Pig tar

One hand grabs my left ankle, and the other my right ear.

It is a fool who shelters doubt harder than ebony.

But whom shall be my judge, I who hath transpired with worms?

I hath no good purpose to peruse the hearts of others.

For my love revealed mine heart to be fouler than pig tar.


The Freedom

A beloved bride most beautiful thy deserved.

Instead thee must content with a pig.

Should I now lament for another thousand years?

Hath thy not commanded for the red links to be made distinct once again?
